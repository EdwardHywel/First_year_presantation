Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR)

What is GFR?

  • Glomerular filtration rate (GFR) describes the flow rate of filtered fluid through the kidney
  • GFR may be accurately calculated by comparative measurements of substances in the blood and urine, or estimated by formulas using just a blood test result

CKD-EPI equation

The most widely used equation is the CKD-EPI equation:

\[ {\scriptstyle \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \log(GFR) &= \log(a) + \log\left(\min\left(\frac{Scr}{b_F},1\right)\right)c_F + \log\left(\max\left(\frac{Scr}{b_F},1\right)\right)d + \log(e)Age + \log(f) && \quad \text{if} \> Sex = F \\ & \\ \log(GFR) &= \log(a) + \log\left(\min\left(\frac{Scr}{b_M},1\right)\right)c_M + \log\left(\max\left(\frac{Scr}{b_M},1\right)\right)d + \log(e)Age && \quad \text{if} \> Sex = M \end{aligned} \end{equation*} } \]
